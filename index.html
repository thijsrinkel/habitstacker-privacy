Privacy Policy

HabitStacker  ·  Last updated: June 2026

HabitStacker is built with your privacy in mind. This policy explains what data the app collects, stores, and shares — in plain language.

What data HabitStacker collects

HabitStacker stores the following data locally on your device only:

  • The stacks and habits you create
  • Completion history and streak counts
  • Reminder times you configure
  • App preferences (sounds on/off, etc.)

This data never leaves your device. It is not sent to any server, not shared with third parties, and not used for advertising.

What HabitStacker does not collect

  • No account or login is required
  • No analytics or crash reporting tools are used
  • No personal information (name, email, location) is collected
  • No data is sold or shared with third parties

Notifications

If you enable reminders, HabitStacker uses iOS local notifications. These are scheduled entirely on your device — no notification data is sent to any server.

Apple Watch & Widget

The Apple Watch app and the Home Screen widget read your habit data from a shared local container on your device. No data leaves your device through these features either.

Children's privacy

HabitStacker does not knowingly collect any information from children under 13. The app collects no personal data from any user.

Changes to this policy

If this policy changes, the updated version will be posted here with a new date. Significant changes will also be noted in the App Store release notes.
